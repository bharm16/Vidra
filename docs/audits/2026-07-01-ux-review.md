# UX Review — 2026-07-01 (headed walkthrough)

**Method:** headed browser, true first-run (storage cleared), walking the golden
path as the ADR-0002 creator ("I don't know what to type"), then every rail
panel and auth. Test-mode boot (GCS still suspended), so the frame step shows
its failure state — itself a reviewed surface. Graded against CLAUDE.md's UX
rules and the CONTEXT.md domain model.

**Verdict:** the bones are good — focused first screen, coherent dark visual
language, excellent expansion output, span highlighting that genuinely aids
comprehension, a fast click-to-enhance tray, clean panel empty-states, a solid
sign-in page. The product's problem is not polish, it's **stagecraft**: the
canvas (the stage) is empty at every moment of the loop, the creator's work
and the product's magic all happen in a chat footer, and the loop's beats
(expand → frame → motion → clip) are never narrated.

---

## A. Structural (design decisions, need owner input)

### A1. The canvas is upside-down — the stage is always empty

At every stage of the loop, ~75% of the viewport is a black void while the
product's core artifact (the expanded prompt, the failed-frame error, the
suggestion tray) stacks up inside the bottom input bar. CONTEXT.md says the
**first frame** "has displaced the text prompt as the center of the product" —
but nothing ever renders on the stage, and the prompt monopolizes the footer.
The empty-state copy even promises "each generation lands on this canvas," and
then nothing ever visibly lands there (frame failure renders as a footer line,
not a canvas state). Decision needed: what owns the canvas at each beat.

### A2. The 10-second void after "Make it"

One second after submit: hero gone, button unchanged, no spinner, no status,
no copy. For the full LLM round-trip (~8–15s) the product's signature moment is
indistinguishable from a hang; double-submit is invited. `IdeaBoxStatusChip`
exists in the codebase but nothing user-visible renders. Decision needed:
whether the loop becomes a visible narrative (stepper/skeleton/stage copy) or
just a spinner.

### A3. The creator's original idea is destroyed on expansion

The one-liner is replaced in-place by the expanded text. No before/after, no
"your idea" anywhere (it survives only as the derived session title). For
trust — "what did you do to my words?" — and for re-expansion, the original
should persist visibly. Adjacent to UX rule #1 (work is never lost).

### A4. First-screen scope for the creator persona

- Example chips are **inert decoration** (styled as pills, not clickable) —
  a looks-clickable trap aimed at exactly the "don't know what to type" user.
- The model chip exposes provider names ("Sora 2") — insider vocabulary in the
  primary bar while model-intelligence is frozen and v1 hardcodes the choice.
- Rail: Chars/Styles/Gallery for a first-run anonymous creator — keep or defer
  is a scope decision.

## B. Bugs / broken affordances (no decision needed — fix)

1. **Gallery panel doesn't open.** Rail icon takes active state; no panel, no
   empty state (Chars/Styles both have proper ones).
2. **Developer chrome in production**: "Copy Debug" / "Copy All Debug" buttons
   in the suggestion tray header, plus an unexplained "6" count badge.
3. **Two competing "Apply" buttons** visible at once in the suggestion tray
   (custom-change Apply + tray-level Apply).
4. **Raw taxonomy ids as user-facing labels**: suggestion chips subtitled
   `shot.type` (internal dot-notation).
5. **Header title never updates**: breadcrumb stays "Untitled" even after the
   session gets a derived title ("Old Fisherman Mending…" appears in the
   Sessions panel but not the header). Plus doubled "Untitled" signals on
   first-run (breadcrumb + hero label).
6. **Rail icons have no labels/tooltips** ("Chars" a11y label is itself
   jargon).
7. Retry-with-no-escalation: "Try again" on frame failure retries identically
   forever; after N failures there's no "something's wrong on our end."
8. Suggestion tray shows a bare horizontal scrollbar under chips.

## C. What's genuinely good (protect these)

- Expansion output quality (post-fallback-fix) is the product's best moment.
- Span highlighting: semantic color-coding reads instantly; the best visual in
  the app.
- Click-to-enhance tray is fast and the "Best" ranking is a nice touch.
- Panel behavior honors UX rule #2 (tools persist; canvas untouched).
- Sign-in page: clean, states its value ("sync prompt history across
  devices").
- Frame-failure error is honest and inline (post-masking-fix).
